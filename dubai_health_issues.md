# Common Health Issues in Dubai/UAE

## Top Health Problems
- Depression (18% of population)
  - Leading cause of disability
  - Symptoms include helplessness, tiredness, loss of interest, aggressive behavior
  - Treatment options include antidepressants and Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation (TMS) therapy
  - Psychiatric treatments often excluded from health insurance coverage

- Injuries (17% of overall deaths in 2010)
  - Common causes: road accidents, falls, drowning
  - Major cause of economic loss (approximately AED 3.5 billion per year)
  - Includes direct and indirect costs of treatment

- Heart Diseases
  - Cardiovascular diseases have increased due to shift from semi-nomadic to urbanized lifestyle
  - UAE patients with cardiovascular conditions live 15 years less compared to Western counterparts
  - Risk factors: obesity, diabetes, high cholesterol, smoking
  - Nearly 9 in 10 people in UAE at risk of cardiovascular disease
  - Treatment costs range from AED 3,000 to AED 60,000+

## Other Significant Health Issues
- Obesity
  - 1 in 5 people in UAE are obese
  - More than 66% men and 60% women are overweight
  - Proportion of obese children is 1.8 times that in the United States
  - Being obese results in higher insurance premiums

- Diabetes
  - 1 in 5 people suffer from diabetes
  - 40% of healthcare spending allocated for diabetes treatment

## Additional Health Statistics
- Children have cholesterol levels similar to 60-year-old men
- Over 40% of adult population have high blood pressure
- 60% of deaths in GCC countries are due to non-communicable diseases (NCDs)
