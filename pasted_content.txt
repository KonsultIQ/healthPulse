Master Question
The challenge addresses that traditional health logistics systems don't adjust to demand in predictive and real-time ways. Your task is to create a solution that answers: How can an agentic AI logistics assistant integrate hospital, GP, and social media data to predict and respond to outbreaks and fluctuations in demand?
Optimization Goals
Your system should be optimized for:

Creating a future where healthcare supply meets real-time demand
Preventing medicine shortages in high-risk areas
Reducing waste by optimizing stock redistribution
Supporting faster response to outbreaks and seasonal health trends
Improving efficiency and cost-effectiveness in healthcare logistics

Key Use Case
"I am a logistics manager for a national pharmacy chain. I need a system that anticipates demand for pharmaceuticals based on real-time health trends."
Processing Requirements
Your solution should be an agentic AI-powered health logistics assistant that:

Integrates hospital, GP, and social media data to predict outbreaks and medicine demand
Automatically adjusts supply chains to ensure the right pharmaceuticals reach the right locations
Minimizes waste by redistributing surplus medicines to high-need areas
Provides insights and reports to stakeholders on health trends and inventory management

Interface Options
Web, mobile, smart wearables
Core Features ("Starters for Ten")

Real-time Health Monitoring: AI analyzes illness trends from multiple data sources, clinical and syndromic
Dynamic Logistics Optimization: Adjusts pharmaceutical distribution automatically
Proactive Public Health Support: Ensures medicine availability before shortages occur

Stretch Goal
Add suppliers data
Special Considerations
Automatically adjusts orders back up the chain
Data Sources
Since real healthcare and logistics data may not be accessible, you're encouraged to:

Synthesize data or find open datasets
Utilize example datasets from Kaggle for healthcare data
Consider potential APIs like WHO API, FDB drug database, mapping APIs, and news APIs

Target Personas

Dr. Ayesha, 42 — Public Health Director, Dubai Health Authority

Profile: Oversees health policy and emergency response
Needs: Real-time data insights to prevent medicine shortages and respond to health spikes
Challenge: Current supply chains can't react fast enough to data from GPs, ERs, and public reports


Imran, 39 — Regional Logistics Manager, Pharmaceutical Distributor

Profile: Manages medicine delivery and stock levels across UAE
Needs: Predictive analytics to optimize delivery routes and stock priorities
Challenge: Misses emerging demand due to outdated reporting cycles


Leila, 31 — Pharmacist in Deira

Profile: Works at a busy community pharmacy
Needs: Wants to avoid running out of common medicines during sudden surges in flu or allergy seasons
Challenge: No visibility into upcoming public health trends



Agentic AI Requirements
Remember that your solution must qualify as true Agentic AI (not just an AI Agent) by exhibiting these four key characteristics:

Perception: Actively gather real-time data from surroundings (sensors, databases, digital interfaces, external sources)
Reasoning: Use advanced reasoning engine (often LLMs) to process and analyze data
Action: Decide and execute actions, integrating with external tools via APIs
Learning: Implement a continuous learning loop to improve over time based on feedback

Judging Criteria
Your solution will be evaluated based on:

Impact (20%): Societal, economic, and environmental benefits
Creativity (20%): Novelty and innovation of the approach
Relevance (20%): How well it addresses the challenge and delivers user value
Technology (30%): Technical feasibility and implementation quality
Presentation (10%): Effectiveness of communication and demonstration
