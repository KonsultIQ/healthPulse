# Dubai Healthcare System Overview

## Structure
- Dubai's healthcare system is regulated at both federal and emirate level
- The Dubai Health Authority (DHA) oversees both public and private healthcare
- DHA administers four public hospitals: Dubai, Rashid, Latifa, and Hatta
- Public healthcare is primarily used by Emirati citizens (15% of population)
- Expats (85% of population) primarily use private healthcare
- There are more than 130 private health facilities in Dubai

## Funding
- Healthcare is funded through taxation and the public sector
- Patient expenditures and co-pays provide additional funding
- Employees with coverage through work pay 20% copayment of medical costs (capped at 1,000 dirhams/year)
- Employers must provide medical insurance for expat employees
- Healthcare expenditure was 50.3 billion dirhams in 2018, expected to rise to 53 billion dirhams
- Dubai doesn't have personal income tax, raising concerns about future funding

## Insurance
- Since 2014, all residents (expats and Emiratis) must have medical insurance
- Companies must provide private health insurance for expat employees
- The Essentials Benefits Plan (EBP) was developed for lower-salary expats
- Common complaint: employer-provided insurance often offers minimal coverage
- Many people purchase top-up premiums for enhanced coverage

## Access to Care
- Standards of care are very high in both public and private systems
- Private facilities offer shorter wait times and greater access to specialists
- To access government-run facilities, residents need a health card
- Application requires Emirates ID, passport, residence visa, photos, tenancy contract, and fees
